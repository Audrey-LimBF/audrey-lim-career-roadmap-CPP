FOLIO 02

Why Am I Here?

Purpose and beliefs
"Large-scale financial decisions — mergers, acquisitions, IPOs, bond issuances — shape entire industries and economies, yet most people never see how those deals actually get made. I want to be at the centre of that process."

I chose Banking & Finance at Ngee Ann Polytechnic over the Junior College route because I already knew the direction I wanted, and I'd rather start building real financial fluency at seventeen than wait three more years to begin. Since enrolling, I've kept a daily habit of tracking financial news and following major M&A activity — not because a module required it, but because understanding how capital actually moves through markets is the foundation everything else in this career sits on.

I'm under no illusion that this path is easy. Investment banking, especially at a firm like J.P. Morgan, sits among the most competitive entry points in the entire finance industry. Getting there from a polytechnic diploma means I have to do more, earlier, than someone starting from a university finance programme — which is exactly why this roadmap exists: not as a wish-list, but as a sequence of specific, achievable steps between where I am now and where I want to end up.

What I believe
  • Competence earns trust before charisma does. In a field built on other people's money, being right and being rigorous matters more than being likeable.
  • Starting early compounds. The advantage of beginning financial literacy at seventeen instead of twenty-one isn't really about the four years — it's about the habits that get to run for longer.
  • Access has to be earned with evidence, not ambition alone. Saying "I want to work in IB" means nothing without a financial model, a CFA paper, or an internship to show for it.
Where I want to be in 5–10 years

In five years, I want to have completed a finance degree at NUS or NTU, finished J.P. Morgan's relevant virtual or apprenticeship pathway, and secured a Summer Analyst placement. In ten, I want to be a working Investment Banking Analyst inside a Corporate & Investment Banking team — not yet at the top, but unmistakably on the way there, with the technical and ethical foundation to be trusted with real client relationships.

Why ethics matter to me here

Investment banking sits close to enormous amounts of other people's capital, and the industry's worst moments — from the 2008 financial crisis onward — have almost always traced back to someone treating integrity as optional under pressure. I want to be the kind of analyst whose numbers can be trusted precisely because cutting a corner was never on the table, even when no one was watching.

How Do I Get There?

Bringing the who and why into being
Long-term target role
Long-Term Career Goal · Degree-Stage

Investment Banking Analyst — J.P. Morgan, Global Investment Banking

Investment Banking Analysts support live client transactions — mergers, acquisitions, leveraged buyouts, and capital markets advisory — by building financial models, analysing market data, and preparing materials used in real pitches and deal execution. This is the role my entire roadmap is built around reaching, and it is reached via a recognised undergraduate or graduate finance degree, typically through J.P. Morgan's Summer Analyst Programme.

Why this, specifically: investment banking sits at the intersection of the things I find most absorbing — quantitative analysis, high-stakes judgment, and direct exposure to how capital actually moves. J.P. Morgan is one of the most selective and prestigious entry points into that work, which makes it the right target to plan backward from, even knowing how competitive it is.

The realistic first step — a job I can actually apply for now
Real, Diploma-Eligible Posting

J.P. Morgan Singapore Apprenticeship Program

Roles & responsibilities: A one-year program for polytechnic graduates, joining an innovative team within one of J.P. Morgan's lines of business in Singapore. Apprentices receive introductory training followed by an ongoing curriculum covering product knowledge, personal development, and professional skills, plus direct mentorship from business leaders on live projects.

Qualifications & experience required: Open to diploma graduates (or final-year diploma students) with no more than two years of work experience. Open to all disciplines — not restricted to Business or Finance — though a Banking & Finance diploma gives a direct advantage for placement onto Corporate & Investment Banking-track teams.

Skills & attributes required: Proficiency in Excel, PowerPoint, and Word; strong attention to detail; comfort working in teams; a highly motivated, "can-do" attitude; the ability to multi-task in a fast-paced environment; strong communication and interpersonal skills.

Outcome: Strong performers may receive offers of full-time employment at the end of the program — making this the realistic bridge between my diploma now and the Analyst role I'm ultimately aiming for.

4a · Course and modules

My Banking & Finance diploma is structured so that each year builds directly toward the analytical and technical core an IB Analyst role requires. Below are the six modules most directly relevant to that goal, drawn from Year 2 and Year 3 of the curriculum.

ModuleYearRelevance to IB Analyst path
Corporate Finance Y2.1 Capital budgeting, capital structure, and business valuation — the direct technical foundation for the financial modelling IB analysts perform daily.
Financial Markets & Instruments Y2.1 How stocks, bonds, and FX instruments function within the market system — essential context for any capital markets advisory work.
Investments Y2.2 Risk/return characteristics and valuation techniques across asset classes — directly transferable to comparable company and DCF analysis.
Corporate & Institutional Banking Y2.2 Commercial lending, transaction management, and credit evaluation — gives me the institutional-banking-side vocabulary IB analysts work alongside.
Risk & Compliance Y2.2 Market and credit risk management, regulatory compliance — the guardrails every real deal has to be checked against.
Applied Project in Finance (Capstone) Y3.1 An industry-based applied project requiring independent research, analysis, and problem-solving — the closest simulation my diploma offers to real deal work.

Beyond the curriculum, I'm planning to use my Year 3 Business Elective slots on AI & Digital Transformation, since fluency with AI-assisted modelling tools is becoming a baseline expectation in modern IB workflows, not just a fintech-specific skill.

Technical Skills Certification

Wall Street Prep / Breaking Into Wall Street (BIWS) — Financial Modelling

Structure: Self-paced, practitioner-built courses in DCF modelling, M&A modelling, and LBO modelling, taught using the same Excel-based techniques used inside real IB teams.

Admission requirements: Open enrolment, paid course — no formal prerequisites beyond basic Excel familiarity.

Why this: Unlike CFA, which is broad and theory-heavy, this is the fastest way to build the exact modelling fluency an IB analyst is expected to have on day one — closing the practical skills gap a diploma alone won't close.
